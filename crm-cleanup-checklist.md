# CRM Cleanup Checklist

## Purpose

A CRM is only valuable when the data is accurate, complete, and trusted.

This checklist helps maintain CRM hygiene and reporting accuracy.

---

## Contact Audit

### Verify

- First Name populated
- Last Name populated
- Email address valid
- Phone number available
- Contact owner assigned

---

## Company Audit

### Verify

- Company name standardized
- Website populated
- Industry populated
- Employee count available
- Revenue range available

---

## Opportunity Audit

### Verify

- Deal value entered
- Expected close date set
- Opportunity owner assigned
- Pipeline stage accurate
- Next step documented

---

## Duplicate Record Review

### Contacts

- Duplicate emails removed
- Duplicate names reviewed

---

### Companies

- Duplicate accounts merged
- Parent-child relationships reviewed

---

## Lifecycle Stage Review

Verify:

- Subscriber
- Lead
- MQL
- SQL
- Opportunity
- Customer

Records should reflect current reality.

---

## Pipeline Hygiene Review

Check:

- Stale opportunities
- Inactive deals
- Missing next steps
- Incorrect stages

---

## Automation Review

Validate:

- Lead routing workflows
- Task creation workflows
- Notifications
- Lifecycle automation

---

## Reporting Review

Verify:

- Dashboard accuracy
- Attribution accuracy
- Forecast reliability
- Conversion reporting

---

## Cleanup Cadence

### Weekly

- Duplicate review
- Missing field review

### Monthly

- Opportunity audit
- Lifecycle audit

### Quarterly

- Full CRM audit
- Reporting review

---

## Key Principle

Clean data creates reliable decisions.

Bad data creates expensive mistakes.
